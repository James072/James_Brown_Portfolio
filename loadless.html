The creation of this app was a result of researching the daily challenges faced by those who have been diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder, level 1, also known as High Functioning Autistic Individuals. The initial thought came from my own son, who also has ASD-1, witnessing the challenges he faced growing up. The purpose of the app is to be a daily tool that helps them navigate the challenging areas of their lives.

The Loadless Neuro Assistant was built to target 3 main areas of daily living in which neurodivergent people experience the most difficulties:

  • Cognitive Load - Dealing with the initiation and completion of daily tasks
  • Social Load - Involves dealing with human interactions in a work or social setting
  • Emotional & Sensory Load - Dealing with intense emotions caused by being overly stimulated due to environmental conditions

The impact to the life of an HFA individual appears as:

  • Procrastination; being stuck on simple tasks
  • Feelings of being "left behind" in conversations
  • Difficulties with networking and building relationships
  • Having intense emotional responses to small stressors

Loadless Neuro Assistant is designed with mobile-first development in mind, to be a tool that helps HFA individuals manage the challenges they face daily. Here's how it works:

The user presses one of three buttons - "Calm", "Okay" or "Overwhelmed" - to input their current state of being.

The app then prompts the user to input a brief description of what they are experiencing, which can be typed, spoken through voice-to-text, or text extracted from an image of a handwritten note or computer screen to be input.

The user's state and the user's description is sent through to the etd Pipeline, which then starts the 3 base prompt engines to process the input and determine the prompt engine that should respond. Once determined, the response from one or more of the base prompt engines is sent to the fusion engine prompt which determines the final output that is sent to the user's screen.
